# $100M Money Models — Framework Distillation

Alex Hormozi's third book (released Aug 2025, sold 2.97M copies on launch day — Guinness record). Core premise: **a deliberate sequence of offers that lets you get more customers and make more per customer.**

---

## What is a Money Model?

> "A Money Model is a deliberate sequence of offers. It's what you offer, when you offer it, and how you offer it — to make as much money as you can as fast as you can."

**The goal:** Make enough money from one customer within 30 days to afford acquiring 2 more customers.

This ratio (sometimes called CAC payback <30 days at 3x) is what unlocks unlimited growth. Once you hit it, you can spend more on ads than competitors and outscale them.

---

## The 3 Stages

Every Money Model sequences 3 stages of offers:

```
Stage I    Stage II          Stage III
─────────  ──────────────    ──────────────
Get Cash   Get More Cash     Get The Most Cash
Attraction → Upsell/Downsell → Continuity
```

---

## Stage I — Attraction Offers

**Purpose:** Convert strangers into first-time customers. Solve the immediate cash problem.

### Key principles:
- Low price point (lowers barrier to entry)
- Delivers massive immediate value
- Designed to be irresistible to your target market
- Profit is NOT the goal at this stage — acquisition is
- Ideally profits just enough to pay for next customer's acquisition

### Types of attraction offers:
1. **Tripwire** — cheap entry product ($7, $27, $47)
2. **Free + shipping** — physical product with shipping cost
3. **Introductory service** — audit, trial, first session
4. **Lead magnet → tripwire** — free thing upgrades to paid
5. **Low-ticket SaaS trial** — $1 first month

### Why Stage I matters:
- A buyer (even of a $7 product) is 10-30x more likely to buy again than a non-buyer
- The #1 predictor of future revenue = has-bought-before vs has-not-bought-before
- This is the gateway. Get them to say YES once.

---

## Stage II — Upsell & Downsell Offers

**Purpose:** Maximize revenue from new customers within the first 30 days.

### Upsell sequence:
- **Immediate upsell** — offered at checkout, before they've even received the first product
- **Post-purchase upsell** — after they've consumed the first offer and gotten a quick win
- **Bump offer** — order bump at checkout ("add this for $19")

### Downsell sequence:
- Offered to people who said NO to the upsell
- Lower price, different format (e.g., self-paced vs group, DIY vs DWY)
- Captures revenue from hesitant buyers who still want something

### Key principles:
- Timing matters — upsells should come immediately (strike while iron is hot)
- Each subsequent offer should solve the NEXT problem the customer has
- Don't re-pitch the same thing — sequence products that build on each other

### Example sequence (coaching business):
1. Attraction: $7 swipe file
2. Upsell 1: $97 mini-course
3. Upsell 2: $997 self-paced program
4. Downsell: $297 eBook + templates (for those who said no to $997)

---

## Stage III — Continuity Offers

**Purpose:** Recurring revenue. Maximize lifetime value. The profit engine.

### Types of continuity:
1. **Subscription** — monthly access to software, content, or community
2. **Membership** — ongoing group program with weekly calls
3. **Maintenance / retainer** — services billed monthly
4. **Usage-based** — pay as you go with monthly minimum
5. **Consumables** — monthly replenishment of a physical product

### Why continuity is the wealth-builder:
- 1 customer on $97/mo continuity for 12 months = $1,164 LTV
- Predictable revenue = you can plan, invest, hire
- Compounds with every new customer added
- The quiet key to every $100M+ business

### Continuity principles:
- Value must RENEW monthly (new content, new results, fresh benefit)
- Onboarding is the #1 lever on retention ("If you want to conquer churn, master onboarding")
- Always have an upgrade path (basic → pro → premium tier)

## Putting it all together — The Model Math

### The 30-day cash flow check:

> If a customer enters your funnel today, how much cash have they generated by day 30?

Target: **2-3x your CAC (customer acquisition cost)**

Example:
- CAC: $200 per customer
- Stage I: $47 (paid)
- Stage II: $497 upsell (30% take rate) = $149 avg
- Stage III: $97/mo continuity (50% stick rate month 1) = $48
- 30-day revenue per customer: $244
- **30-day ratio: 1.22x CAC** — needs optimization

Fix: raise tripwire, improve upsell conversion, add a bump offer.

Once ratio is 2-3x, you have **unlimited ad budget** — reinvest to scale.

## The "Money Model" test

If your current offer structure can't answer "YES" to all 3:

1. Does Stage I make first-sale friction as low as possible?
2. Does Stage II maximize revenue in the first 30 days?
3. Does Stage III create predictable recurring revenue?

...then you have an offer, not a Money Model. **Fix the gap.**

---

## Common Money Model mistakes

- Only having one offer (no sequence)
- Upsells that have nothing to do with the attraction offer
- Making continuity too expensive too early
- Forgetting downsells (losing 30-50% of potential revenue)
- Not measuring 30-day cash per customer (flying blind)
- Scaling paid ads before the model ratio works (burning cash)

---

## The compounding effect

Money Models compound because:
- Stage I gets you customers
- Stage II pays for more Stage I
- Stage III makes you profitable long-term
- Every new customer = compounding MRR (through Stage III)

Get the sequence right → you have a business.
Get it wrong → you have an expensive hobby.
