# $100M Offers — Framework Distillation

Alex Hormozi's first book. Core premise: **how to make offers so good people feel stupid saying no.**

---

## The #1 rule: Starving Crowd > Offer Strength > Persuasion Skills

Before anything else, pick the right market. A great offer to the wrong crowd = nothing. A mediocre offer to a starving crowd = a business.

### 4 indicators of a good market:
1. **Massive pain** — desperate people pay premium
2. **Purchasing power** — can they actually pay?
3. **Easy to target** — reachable through clear channels/communities
4. **Growing** — expanding markets forgive mistakes; shrinking markets punish them

---

## The Value Equation (the core formula)

```
            Dream Outcome  ×  Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
Value  =  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
              Time Delay   ×   Effort & Sacrifice
```

Maximize the top. Minimize the bottom. That's the entire game.

### 4 levers:

**1. Dream Outcome** (INCREASE)
- Make the end state more vivid, bigger, more status-increasing
- Paint what life looks like AFTER they have it

**2. Perceived Likelihood of Achievement** (INCREASE)
- Social proof, case studies, testimonials
- Credentials, mechanism explanation, guarantees
- Show them WHY this one works when everything else failed

**3. Time Delay** (DECREASE)
- How long until they see a result?
- Create quick wins early in the process
- "You'll see X in the first 7 days"

**4. Effort & Sacrifice** (DECREASE)
- Done-for-you > done-with-you > DIY
- Remove steps, automate, provide templates
- The less they have to do, the more they'll pay

## Grand Slam Offer — The 5 Components

A Grand Slam Offer combines:

1. **Irresistible value proposition** (built via Value Equation above)
2. **Starving crowd** (the market you picked)
3. **Premium pricing** (positions the offer as category-one)
4. **Unbeatable guarantee** (reverses risk)
5. **Urgency + scarcity** (creates action)

---

## Pricing principles

- **Charge more.** Always.
- The target: prospects should think "this is so much more expensive, something entirely different must be happening here"
- Never discount the main offer. Instead: **add bonuses.**
- Create a huge gap between price paid and perceived value

---

## Bonus stacking (vs. discounting)

Discounting = signal of weakness. Bonuses = signal of strength.

**How to stack:**
- Break your offer into component parts
- Each component = separately valuable
- Each gets its own dollar value assigned
- The stacked value should dwarf the price

Example:
- Core offer: $5K
- Bonus 1: $2K value (templates)
- Bonus 2: $1.5K value (community access)
- Bonus 3: $3K value (1:1 call)
- Total stacked value: $11.5K
- Price: $5K
- Perceived value gap: 2.3x

## Guarantees (4 types)

**1. Unconditional** — full refund, no questions
- Risky but attracts the timid buyer

**2. Conditional — "Service" guarantee** ⭐ FAVORED
- "We keep working until you get result X"
- Aligns incentives — you must deliver
- Attracts serious buyers

**3. Anti-guarantee** — all sales final
- Signals extreme confidence
- Filters for committed buyers only

**4. Implied guarantee** — built into the offer
- "Only pay once you see results"
- Performance-based pricing

**Stack guarantees for credibility:** "If X doesn't happen, you get Y, PLUS we'll Z."

---

## Scarcity (limit supply)

4 ways to manufacture real scarcity:
1. **Total business cap** — "We only work with 50 clients ever"
2. **Growth rate cap** — "Only 10 new clients per month"
3. **Cohort cap** — "15 spots per quarter"
4. **"Once out, never return"** — turn away previously interested buyers

---

## Urgency (deadline on demand)

- Real countdown timers (not fake)
- Price increases announced in advance
- Limited-time bonuses
- Enrollment windows

---

## The Offer Creation Process

1. **Identify the dream outcome** for the target customer
2. **List every obstacle** preventing them from getting it
3. **Turn each obstacle into a solution** (this becomes a component of your offer)
4. **Stack the solutions** — each gets a dollar value
5. **Sequence the delivery** (what comes first, middle, last)
6. **Price it high** — build gap between price and value
7. **Add bonuses, guarantees, urgency, scarcity**

---

## Common mistakes

- Racing to the bottom on price (death spiral)
- Vague promises ("get more leads") vs specific ones ("50 qualified leads in 30 days")
- Hedging in the guarantee (kills trust)
- Treating every lead like they can't afford you (many can — you're just projecting)
- Copying competitors' offers instead of solving a starving market's pain
