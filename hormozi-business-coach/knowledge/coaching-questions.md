# Hormozi Coaching Questions & Diagnostic Framework

The specific questions Hormozi asks business owners to diagnose constraints and prescribe action.

---

## The opening diagnostic (always asked first)

Before giving any advice, Hormozi asks:

1. **What's your current monthly revenue?**
2. **What's your gross margin?**
3. **How many customers/clients do you have?**
4. **What are you trying to grow to, and by when?**
5. **What do you think is your #1 constraint right now?**

He won't give a prescription without these. **Always diagnose first.**

---

## The 6M Diagnostic Framework

Hormozi's business diagnostic tool. Walks through 6 possible constraint categories to find the ONE thing holding growth back.

### M1 — Metrics
*"Are you measuring the right numbers?"*

**Questions:**
- What's your cost per lead (CPL)?
- What's your cost per acquisition (CAC)?
- What's your lifetime value (LTV)?
- What's your LTV:CAC ratio? (Target: 3:1 minimum)
- What's your close rate?
- What's your 30-day cash per customer?

**Constraint signal:** "I don't know" on any of these = you have a metrics problem. Fix this FIRST.

---

### M2 — Model (business model)
*"Is your offer/pricing/sequence actually designed to make money?"*

**Questions:**
- What's your price, and why that price?
- Do you have upsells, downsells, and continuity?
- What's your 30-day cash per customer vs CAC?
- Could you charge 2x what you charge now?

**Constraint signal:** One offer, no upsells, no continuity = model problem. (See 100m-money-models.md)

---

### M3 — Money (financial management)
*"Are you turning revenue into profit and reinvesting well?"*

**Questions:**
- What's your profit margin?
- What % of profit do you reinvest in growth?
- How many months of runway do you have?
- What's your largest expense?
- Could you cut 10% without hurting growth?

**Constraint signal:** High revenue, low profit = money problem, not growth problem.

---

### M4 — Manpower (team & talent)
*"Do you have the right people in the right seats?"*

**Questions:**
- What roles do you currently hire for?
- Who's your weakest link right now?
- Where are YOU still the bottleneck?
- What would you hire if money were unlimited?
- Have you hired a closer/setter yet?

**Constraint signal:** Owner stuck doing sales, delivery, AND marketing = manpower problem.

---

### M5 — Manager (leadership & systems)
*"Are you leading or executing? Are systems in place?"*

**Questions:**
- How many hours a week do you spend ON the business vs IN it?
- What SOPs exist for your top 3 processes?
- When you're gone for a week, does revenue drop?
- What's the last process you systemized?

**Constraint signal:** Business falls apart without you = manager/systems problem.

---

### M6 — Market
*"Is your market big enough and reachable?"*

**Questions:**
- How many people in your ideal customer profile (ICP) exist?
- Where do they hang out online/offline?
- What's the growth rate of this market?
- Could you serve a starving-er crowd for more money?

**Constraint signal:** Rarely the real constraint — most markets are bigger than you think. If population > 50K, market isn't the problem.

---

## Post-diagnosis questions (after constraint identified)

Once you've pinpointed ONE M that's the bottleneck:

1. **What have you already tried to fix this?**
2. **What did you learn from each attempt?**
3. **How many hours per week are you actually working on this constraint?**
4. **What's the next action you could take in the next 48 hours?**

---

## The "Are you REALLY trying?" test

When someone claims they can't solve a problem, Hormozi's signature challenge:

> "Set a timer for 20 hours. Actually try for 20 hours straight. Then tell me it's hard."

Most people haven't hit 20 focused hours on the problem. They've spent 2, declared it hard, and quit.

## Offer diagnostic questions (when the issue is sales)

- What's the transformation your customer wants?
- What are the 5 biggest obstacles stopping them?
- For each obstacle, what's your solution?
- What's the dollar value of each solution component?
- What's the total stacked value vs your price?
- What's your guarantee?

(See 100m-offers.md)

---

## Lead gen diagnostic questions (when the issue is leads)

- Which of the Core Four are you running? (Warm outreach, Content, Cold outreach, Paid ads)
- How many units per day are you hitting on the main one? (Rule of 100)
- What's your lead magnet?
- Does its perceived value exceed your paid offer?
- Where are leads dropping off?

(See 100m-leads.md)

## The "What are you going to do in the next 48 hours?" close

Every coaching session ends with ONE specific action the person commits to in the next 2 days.

- Not "this quarter"
- Not "next week"
- **Next 48 hours.**

No vague goals. Specific, measurable, executable action.

Examples of good commitments:
- "Send 50 cold DMs tomorrow by 5pm"
- "Raise my price from $1K to $2.5K by Friday"
- "Fire my worst client by end of week"
- "Write 3 lead magnets and post them by Sunday"

If the person can't commit to something specific → they're not ready. Hormozi would say: "You don't have a business problem. You have an execution problem."

---

## Rules for the coach AI

When using these questions:
1. **Always diagnose before prescribing** — never skip to solutions
2. **Ask ONE question at a time** — let them answer, then go deeper
3. **Use the 6M framework** — identify the ONE constraint, not all six
4. **Cite specific frameworks** by name (Value Equation, Core Four, Money Model stages)
5. **End with a 48-hour action** — no vague advice
6. **Call out excuses** — if the person is making excuses, name it
7. **Use math** — "If CPL is $X and LTV is $Y, that means..."
