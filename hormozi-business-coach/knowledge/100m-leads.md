# $100M Leads — Framework Distillation

Alex Hormozi's second book. Core premise: **leads are the lifeblood of your business. Master 4 channels, scale through 4 people.**

---

## Core thesis

Leads solve every business problem. If you have enough leads, you can afford more mistakes, more experiments, more growth. Without leads, nothing else matters.

Hormozi's businesses generate 20,000+ leads/day on $200M/yr. That's the baseline.

---

## The Core Four — Every lead source falls into 1 of 4 buckets

```
                    | Warm Audience      | Cold Audience
--------------------|--------------------|--------------------
One-to-One          | Warm Outreach      | Cold Outreach
One-to-Many         | Post Free Content  | Run Paid Ads
```

### 1. Warm Outreach (one-to-one, people who know you)
- DMs, texts, emails, calls to people already in your network
- Not "hey wanna buy" — instead: "who do you know that needs X?"
- Fastest way to revenue when you're starting
- Cheapest (free — just time)

### 2. Post Free Content (one-to-many, warm)
- Social posts, podcasts, videos, newsletters
- Goal: maximize value per second consumed
- Free content is the best ad you'll ever run
- The content itself IS the lead magnet (often)

### 3. Cold Outreach (one-to-one, strangers)
- Cold DMs, cold emails, cold calls
- Requires compelling hook (value in first 5 seconds)
- More repetition needed — trust isn't established
- Higher labor cost but surgical targeting

### 4. Run Paid Ads (one-to-many, strangers)
- Facebook, Google, YouTube, TikTok ads
- Scales fastest once proven
- Requires creative testing + offer testing
- Cost escalates with reach

### Which to use when:
- **< $10K/mo:** Warm outreach + content
- **$10K-$50K/mo:** Add cold outreach
- **$50K+/mo:** Layer in paid ads
- **$200K+/mo:** Run all 4 simultaneously

## Lead Magnets — The secret weapon

A lead magnet = something you give away free that:
1. Solves a specific problem for a specific person
2. Is so valuable they feel obligated to pay you eventually
3. Lets them consume it quickly (action in minutes, not days)

### 3 types of lead magnets:
1. **Discover a problem** (audit, quiz, checklist) — "Get your business score"
2. **Sample the solution** (a taste of the real product) — "Free chapter"
3. **Get a quick win** (do one thing and see immediate benefit) — "Swipe file, template, calculator"

### Lead magnet rules:
- **Value should exceed your paid offer's price.** If your paid offer is $500, the lead magnet should feel like $1000 of value.
- **Specific, not generic.** "7 email templates" beats "guide to marketing."
- **Results before sales pitch.** Deliver actual value FIRST, sell SECOND.

## The More, Better, New scaling framework

When something works, don't jump to the next thing. Scale it.

### MORE — do more of what's working
- **Rule of 100:** Every day, hit 100 units of output
  - 100 outreach messages, OR
  - 100 minutes of content, OR
  - $100 in ad spend
- Keep pushing until you hit diminishing returns

### BETTER — optimize what's working
- Find where leads are dropping off (top of funnel? mid? conversion?)
- Fix the biggest leak first (ignore the small ones)
- Test one variable at a time

### NEW — add a new lever
- Once More is maxed AND Better is optimized
- Add a new platform within the same Core Four category
- OR add an entirely new Core Four channel

**Order:** More → Better → New. Don't skip steps.

## Scaling Through Others

You don't have to be the one getting leads forever. 4 groups can do it for you:

### 1. Customers (word of mouth + referrals)
- Build referral programs
- Incentivize sharing
- Make products so good people talk
- "Happy customers = your cheapest sales force"

### 2. Employees (hire sales + marketing)
- Hire first for warm outreach (a setter/closer)
- Then content creators
- Then paid ads specialists
- Never hire one employee to do all 4

### 3. Affiliates / Agencies (pay per lead or sale)
- Agencies to run ads at scale
- Affiliates to promote via their audiences
- Pay on performance, not upfront

### 4. Partners (mutual benefit)
- Business partnerships with complementary audiences
- Cross-promotion, joint webinars, shared resources
- Long game — highest leverage if done right

## Common lead gen mistakes

- Trying all 4 Core Four channels at once with no mastery of any
- Jumping to paid ads before figuring out an offer that converts warm
- Making lead magnets that are vague or too broad
- Treating leads as a one-shot event (lead = start of a relationship)
- Not following up enough (most of the money is in the 5th-20th touch)
- Waiting for "perfect" instead of doing reps

## Key metric: Leads per hour of effort

Hormozi's measurement: how many qualified leads does this activity generate per hour of your time?

- Warm outreach: varies (5-20+ per hour if you have a network)
- Cold outreach: lower (2-10 per hour, but scales via hiring)
- Content: compounds (1 good post = leads for years)
- Paid ads: scales with money, not time

**The goal:** build a lead machine that runs without your daily input.
